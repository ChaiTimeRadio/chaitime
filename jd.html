ChaiTime with Poet and Writer Jay Deshpande

In this ChaiTime show, we talk to Jay Deshpande about his journey as a poet and writer and listen to some of the poems from his new book, Love the Stranger (YesYes Books, 2015).

Jay Deshpande has held residencies at the Saltonstall Arts Colony and the Vermont Studio Center. He was a fellowship finalist for the Fine Arts Work Center in Provincetown, and was selected by Billy Collins for the 2015 Scotti Merrill Memorial Award at the Key West Literary Seminar. His poems have appeared in Narrative, Boston Review, Sixth Finch, Atlas Review, Handsome, Spork, Prelude, and elsewhere. He holds degrees from Harvard and Columbia. Born in Austin, Texas, he now lives in Brooklyn.

Jay also writes about literature, popular culture, and the arts. He has written extensively for Slate; his essays and reviews also appear in The New Republic, Boston Review, The Millions, Coldfront, Publishers Weekly, and elsewhere.

Additionally, Jay is a seasoned reporter with expertise in the fashion, business, and technology beats. He is a former associate editor of WatchTime, the nationâ€™s top magazine for luxury wristwatch collectors.

Jay has taught writing at Columbia University and workshops for the Academy of American Poets. Among other hats, he has been a tutor in Boston, an English instructor in Paris, and a guitar teacher in Cairo.
